Plans for Summer!

> Progression through the pandemic

Posted by Christopher Swayne on Tuesday, May 26, 2020

What a way to end the school year, or I guess, way to start the year. This spring semester has been rather hectic, as everyone is aware of what has gone on. However, despite this tricky semester we’ve had at University, I’ve been able to hold steady and keep learning through challenging circumstances. Remote learning is fun at first, but it’s not great nor easy long term. Scheduling time to finish assignments as well as study while sitting at the same desk in the same room almost every hour for every day wears you out.

I did however, do my best this semester to enjoy what time I had, given the circumstances, to build with what I could. I’ve laid the foundation of an Android app through my Foundations of Software Engineering course. The internet has also been a great tool for many more to use to explore, and learn even more new things. I, out of a desire for a Unix derived operating system - and wanting to use some hardware besides a MacBook Pro, spent some time to begin learning BSD (Berkley Software Distribution), which as of now is still difficult to fully understand as I’ve become extremely used to Windows and OS X.

This semester has also seen me face even more challenges with time management and organization, that I’ve started to properly manage my time, and search for ways to better my methods. Journaling, reflecting upon my day, my thoughts and my problems, has given me at least some better perceived control over what I’ve been doing, what I need to do, and where to go from point A to B. Due to plenty of things happening over this summer for my family and I, by using a checklist of projects - I can keep track of goals and stuff to complete.

Summer 2020 Goals

  • Digial Office Organization
  • Asymmetrical Android App which can be found here
  • Convert Asymmetrical Android to iOS
  • Repair retro CD Player
  • Read at least 4 books
  • Home “Multi-use” Server

Why have I set these goals for myself?

Mostly because I want to be more productive, and more proactive with my interests, instead of only working and coming home to sleep, or play games like I’ve done in years past. While I do enjoy working and my job itself, there is a desire that I’ve had prop up over the past couple years to do… well… do more. While I certainly know that I’m not really going to make music, paint, or become some master of all trades, I want to try things that interest me in my free time more, instead of doing the same things over again since I was growing up. However, the goals above aren’t the only things that I want to do, there are more things, though not always easy to remember off the top of my head, or ideas that I’ll come up with over the course of this summer, that I’ll want to complete, or write down to post somewhere.

Here will be that somewhere - below, in a separate list will hold both lower priority projects, and new ideas.

Summer 2020 Fresh Ideas

  • Try making a song
  • Try creating a couple How to videos
  • Take a trip to two where I can experiment more with photography - My Flickr

Final Word

As my goals are reached or changed, I’ll continue to update them here, with a check box.

  • like this

I will not be deleting anything here if I fail to meet my goals, rather I’ll use a strike-through, like this, and ideally, each project will have it’s own post as they’re worked on, and completed. This first post of the summer will only be for keeping track, and as an overall guide for my summer to share with you all! If you have any questions, you can contact me on Twitter, Keybase, or via email if you ask.
