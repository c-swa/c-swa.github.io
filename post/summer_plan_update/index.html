Summer Plan Update!

> Reviewing goals, and progress

Posted by Christopher Swayne on Tuesday, June 23, 2020

One Month In

It’s been a while since I last posted an update, so I felt one should be in order. Life during the pandemic hasn’t changed much, and we’ve all still been spending our time with loved ones at home. I’m still working, as I’ve been lucky enough to be considered an essential worker. Especially due to the time I’ve spent between work and home, I haven’t really been able to delve signifcantly into my goals, aside from a few things.

Completed Projects

I did end up working on plenty of more things than the goals I had laid out for myself in the previous post a month ago. At home, there are now security cameras that I’ve installed and set up. I’ve also managed to get altstore.io (obtainable here) to work on my iPhone, and function for me to be able to build and run my own applications on my phone indefinitely.

My Dell Inspiron N5110 was also upgraded, mostly to transition towards using it as my daily “powerful” laptop, as it now has both a better i7 processor and twice as much RAM as my MacBook Pro. However, while the whole story will be put into a separate blog post in the near future, I’ll delve into a few things here.

Upgrading? to an older Dell

I predominantly wanted to have a laptop that was easily serviceable, powerful enough, and reliable to last me the medium term lifespan I need out of it. The MacBook Pro I have, is a fantastic machine, and easily one of the top recommendations I can give for a laptop to most people. However, Apple has been steering away for a long time now away from user service, and building reliable products. The current variation of the MacBook Pro (2016-2019) have a fatal keyboard issue, soldered-on RAM, soldered-on storage, and no user serviceable parts. Pre-2019 models also have a failing display cable, where you get a “stage light” effect from the backlight before the display completely fails.

This Dell that I have, I bought brand new in 2011/2012 I have never had the keyboard fail on me (and neither has the 2015 MacBook’s keyboard for that matter) I did however, have multiple hard disk, and solid state drives fail. No storage solution is permanent. Laptops aren’t either. However, a laptop should be able to have it’s internals replaced over time when they fail to keep the device going. Much like a Ship of Theseus, is it the same device if everything has been replaced?

Incomplete Projects

  • Digital Office Organization
  • Asymmetrical found here
  • Repair CD Player

Why are these incomplete?

Much of my time this month was taken up in part by other unlisted “projects” whether that be related to the listed completed projects, or not. For example, the home server that I wanted to build, one of the reasons was to have a local storage on the network (secured, obviously) to be able to allow all household members to have access to the storage. The security camera system we ended up going with, makes this more difficult, and partially unnecessary, since the system is setup to record to a local drive, and we can obtain the footage simply by phyiscally accessing the device. Hence, part of the reason of having the home server is deviated to another project.

I think that’ll wrap it up for today. This week, I’ll have an update on the Linux laptop that I’ve got plans for.
