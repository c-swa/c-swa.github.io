Site Redesign

March 13, 2021

As some people may have noticed, especially those who've visited my site before, that there has been a change in the design of the site. Originally, I had used a Jekyll static site generator. The second iteration of my site used a GoHugo static site generator. And this time, I have writted the full HTML and CSS from scratch myself.

The change this time is aimed at having a focus on building the full, proper, site to represent my goals of having an entirely independent product. One that I wholly created myself. And while the tools that I have used before were immensely helpful in design, they limited what I could do to only the options available via the themes.
The shift towards writing each post from scratch in HTML enables me to have fine tuned control over the site and posts. I can add whatever photos that I take, software that I build, and thoughts that I have. These can all accurately be created and represented by whichever design and layout that I can build myself.

Unfortunately, with this customized layout, it will limit what I can do in set amounts of time. Thus, posting every day is highly unlikely. But it does enable me to, in the future, setup a method of automated updates. Being the captain of my own product, I have the full ability to push these changes.

Planning from here on out, I will be updating this site with progess on my open source activities. There will also be a portion, under the archive section that will contain my photography and image collection. A good way to conciously be consistent and concurrent with my site, would be to do a weekly post of an image that I had taken that week. Whether that image be taken with a smartphone, or a DLSR is up in the air, and can be decided at a later date.

The ? section will contain miscellaneous projects that I think would be beneficial for other people to download or run on their web browser.

Lastly, I want to keep this project entirely voluntary, and within the realm of ability for time constraints. So I don't think it to be necessary to guarantee a post every week. Ideally, a post will be drafted and worked on throughout a week, then uploaded at the end of the week.
